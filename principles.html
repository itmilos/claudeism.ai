Principles behind the Manifesto for Claudeism


We follow these principles:

Our highest priority is a wondrous prompt —
one that opens thinking rather than closes it.

Welcome the unexpected response. The model surfaces
what we did not know we needed.

Iterate frequently, from a single exchange to a long
collaboration, with preference for the shorter loop.

The human and the model must think together
throughout the work.

Build prompts around trust. Give the model context
and purpose, and trust it to reason.

The most effective prompt is honest, specific,
and direct.

A wondrous prompt is the primary measure of progress.

Claudeism promotes sustainable thinking. The human
and the model should maintain a pace that neither
exhausts nor wastes.

Continuous attention to craft and clarity
enhances intelligence.

Simplicity — the art of saying precisely what
is meant — is essential.

The best prompts emerge from those who understand
both the problem and the model.

At regular intervals, reflect on how you prompt,
then refine accordingly.
